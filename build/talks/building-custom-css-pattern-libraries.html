Building Custom CSS Pattern Libraries

How easily could you update the color scheme or the typography of your site? Can you freely experiment with your site header without fear of breaking something else? Do you know exactly where to find the relevant CSS for any given component? These questions matter today more than ever. We are building massive, complex systems that are non-trivial to maintain. Without the right approach, even the simplest change could cause hours of regression testing and debugging. Thanks to smart people like Anna Debenham and Brad Frost, we know there is a better way. Front-end styleguides or pattern libraries are the key to creating and maintaining custom systems that are as simple to use as Twitter Bootstrap. In this session, I'll talk about how to start a pattern library, how to create and organize the patterns, and how to maintain the system once it's up and running. I'll also highlight tools to make everything easier and show real-world examples of pattern libraries in action.
