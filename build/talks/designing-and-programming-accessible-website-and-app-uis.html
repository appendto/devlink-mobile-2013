Designing and Programming Accessible Website and App UIs

Thursday
2:30 PM – 3:45 PM
Meeting Room E

All too often, popular websites and apps fail to design for accessibility, unknowingly resulting in a huge loss of potential customers who cannot consume or access the site. The reason is that all too often due to overestimating the amount of work it takes to make a website accessible. Developers also underestimate the sheer volume of users that require accessible content, estimated at 20% of the population by WebAIM (a non-profit dedicated to accessibility). In this session, you'll learn how to design websites and code using techniques that enable those with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive impairments to access your websites and use your apps. This includes learning how to use plain old HTML with accessibility in mind, and using ARIA elements, as well as some design practices to keep in mind while laying out webpages. Upon completing this session you will have learned to design sites and apps with better UXs that have the farthest reaching user base.
